# dysentery-symptom-guidance-app

The **Dysentery Symptom Guidance App** is a Streamlit-based educational and research prototype designed to provide symptom-based guidance for dysentery-like illness. The app allows users to enter common clinical features such as blood in stool, mucus in stool, fever, abdominal pain, vomiting, dehydration, stool frequency, age group, and known stool test results.

Based on these inputs, the app provides a provisional classification such as probable bacillary dysentery/shigellosis, possible amoebic dysentery/amoebiasis, other infectious bloody diarrhoea, or unclassified diarrhoeal illness. It also identifies important red-flag symptoms that may require urgent medical attention.

The app provides evidence-informed supportive-care guidance, including oral rehydration, fluid intake, monitoring for dehydration, and medical consultation advice. It also includes safety cautions against self-medication with antibiotics or gut-slowing medicines in suspected bloody diarrhoea.

## Key Features

- Symptom-based dysentery screening
- Provisional classification of dysentery-like illness
- Red-flag identification
- Supportive-care guidance
- Medical review recommendation
- Educational disclaimer
- Simple web interface built with Streamlit

## Intended Use

This app is intended for:

- Teaching and demonstration
- Public-health education
- Clinical decision-support prototyping
- Bioinformatics and digital-health workflow development

## Disclaimer

This app is for educational and research purposes only. It does not diagnose disease, prescribe medicines, or replace consultation with a registered medical practitioner. Patients with bloody diarrhoea, dehydration, high fever, severe abdominal pain, persistent symptoms, pregnancy, immunocompromised status, or illness in children or elderly individuals should seek medical care.
